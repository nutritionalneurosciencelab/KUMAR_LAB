Research
Ongoing Projects

Our lab pursues three interconnected research areas that together address how nutrition and metabolism shape neuroimmune health and disease.

Neuroimmunometabolic Reprogramming

We investigate how shifts in cellular and systemic metabolism reprogram neuroimmune responses — exploring the metabolic checkpoints, signalling cascades, and immune–brain axes that govern neurological health and disease susceptibility.

Neuroimmunology · Immunometabolism

Nutritional Interventions & Metabolic Therapies

We study how ketogenic diets and specific nutritional strategies modulate brain immunity, stress, and aging — investigating the therapeutic potential of dietary regimens and bioactive compounds across neurological and metabolic conditions.

Nutritional Neuroscience · Precision Nutrition
🧠

Neurobiology of Compulsive Eating & Food Preference

We explore the neural and immune mechanisms that drive compulsive eating behaviours and food preference — examining how brain reward circuits, neuroimmune signalling, and metabolic states interact to shape feeding behaviour and its disorders.

Food Behaviour · Neuroimmune Circuits

Oct 29 – Nov 1, 2025 Conference
NeuroMeT Lab at XLIII Annual Meeting of Indian Academy of Neurosciences — IAN 2025
Ms. Arunima Das and Ms. Gauri Chaudhari attended the XLIII Annual Meeting of the Indian Academy of Neurosciences (IAN-2025) — an International Conference on Neurodevelopment and Brain Disorders: Research Trends, Technology and Translation, organised by BRIC–Rajiv Gandhi Centre for Biotechnology, Kovalam, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala.

Both scholars had the opportunity to present their posters, receive valuable feedback from leading neuroscientists, and forge new collaborations. Gauri also participated in a hands-on Single-Cell Sequencing: Library Preparation and Data Analysis workshop, gaining practical skills in cell isolation, library preparation, data preprocessing, and single-cell clustering and visualisation using R.

A truly inspiring and skill-building experience that added meaningful value to their research journeys!
Arunima Das Gauri Chaudhari Dr. Mohit Kumar

July 8–11, 2025 Conference
NeuroMeT Lab at XVII European Meeting on Glial Cells in Health and Disease
Congratulations! Ms. Babita Bhatt and Ms. Chitralekha Gusain successfully presented the lab's work at the XVII European Meeting on Glial Cells in Health and Disease — Glia 2025, Marseille, France.

Babita presented a poster on "Reprogramming Microglial Health: Potential of β-Hydroxybutyrate Under Glucose Deprivation", showcasing the lab's work on metabolic reprogramming of microglia at BRIC-NABI.
