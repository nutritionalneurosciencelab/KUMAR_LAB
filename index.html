Dr. Mohit Kumar

Scientist C  ·  BRIC-NABI, Mohali

Dr. Mohit Kumar leads the Nutritional Neuroscience & Neuroinflammation Laboratory at BRIC-National Agri-Food and Biomanufacturing Institute (NABI), Mohali, where he joined as Scientist C in January 2023. His research spans nutritional neuroscience, neuroinflammation, innate immunity, and immunometabolism — with a focus on how dietary patterns reprogram neuroimmune circuits to drive or protect against metabolic and neurological disorders.

He identified key neuroinflammatory drivers of sugar cravings as potential targets for food addiction and mood disorders, championed sulphur-based nutraceuticals for managing hyperhomocysteinemia-related brain conditions, and demonstrated microglia as precision targets for nicotine cessation therapy. He also showed myricetin — a dietary flavonoid — as a candidate therapeutic for Huntington's Disease.

Research
What We Study

Our lab pursues two interconnected research programmes that together address how nutrition and immunity collaborate to shape neurological health and disease.

⚗️

Immunometabolic Reprogramming for Immunity & Mental Health

Cellular metabolism is central to immune activation — immune cells undergo metabolic reprogramming as they shift from resting to active states. We study how these metabolic checkpoints govern immunity in infection, cancer, and neurological disorders, and develop therapeutic diet plans combining nutritional ketosis and dietary restriction to treat inflammatory and neurological conditions.

🧠

Immunometabolic Correlates of Compulsive & Binge Eating

Ultra-processed food drives dopamine-mediated hedonic overeating cycles, food addiction, and maladaptive neuroadaptations. We recently discovered neuroimmune mechanisms underlying sugar dependence, and now work to identify molecular drivers of compulsive eating to develop precision metabolism-based therapies for diet-associated metabolic and neurological disorders.

🔬

Neuroinflammation & Glial Biology

Microglia — the brain's resident immune cells — are central targets in our work. We probe how dietary and metabolic cues modulate microglial activation, blood-brain barrier integrity, and neuroimmune signalling in models of addiction, metabolic disease, and neurodegeneration.

🥗

Therapeutic Diets & Sex-Specific Effects

Sex profoundly shapes dietary response. We study sex-specific effects of the ketogenic diet and sucrose withdrawal on anxiety-like behaviour and neuroimmune response, informing the development of personalised, sex-aware therapeutic diet strategies.

🌿

Nutraceuticals & Bioactive Compounds

We characterise dietary bioactives — myricetin, hydrogen sulphide donors, sulphur-based nutraceuticals — as therapeutic candidates for Huntington's Disease, depression, anxiety, and cognitive deficits linked to metabolic dysregulation and neuroinflammation.

🎯

Precision Nutrition & Metabolic Therapeutics

Accounting for genetics (e.g., MTHFR polymorphisms), sex, and metabolic state, we design evidence-based precision nutrition strategies to modulate immunity in inflammatory, neurological, and metabolic disorders.
