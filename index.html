Dr. Mohit Kumar

Scientist C  ·  BRIC-NABI, Mohali

Dr. Mohit Kumar leads the Nutritional Neuroscience & Neuroinflammation Laboratory at BRIC-National Agri-Food and Biomanufacturing Institute (NABI), Mohali, where he joined as Scientist C in January 2023. His research spans nutritional neuroscience, neuroinflammation, innate immunity, and immunometabolism — with a focus on how dietary patterns reprogram neuroimmune circuits to drive or protect against metabolic and neurological disorders.

He identified key neuroinflammatory drivers of sugar cravings as potential targets for food addiction and mood disorders, championed sulphur-based nutraceuticals for managing hyperhomocysteinemia-related brain conditions, and demonstrated microglia as precision targets for nicotine cessation therapy. He also showed myricetin — a dietary flavonoid — as a candidate therapeutic for Huntington's Disease.

Research
What We Study

Our lab pursues two interconnected research programmes that together address how nutrition and immunity collaborate to shape neurological health and disease.

⚗️

Immunometabolic Reprogramming for Immunity & Mental Health

Cellular metabolism is central to immune activation. We study metabolic checkpoints that govern immunity in infection, cancer, and neurological disorders, and develop therapeutic diet plans combining nutritional ketosis and dietary restriction.

Immunometabolism
🧠

Immunometabolic Correlates of Compulsive & Binge Eating

Ultra-processed food drives dopamine-mediated hedonic overeating cycles. We discovered neuroimmune mechanisms underlying sugar dependence and work to identify molecular drivers of compulsive eating for precision metabolism-based therapies.

Food Addiction
🔬

Neuroinflammation & Glial Biology

Microglia — the brain's resident immune cells — are central targets. We probe how dietary and metabolic cues modulate microglial activation, blood-brain barrier integrity, and neuroimmune signalling in models of addiction and neurodegeneration.

Microglia · BBB
🥗

Therapeutic Diets & Sex-Specific Effects

Sex profoundly shapes dietary response. We study sex-specific effects of the ketogenic diet and sucrose withdrawal on anxiety-like behaviour and neuroimmune response, informing sex-aware therapeutic diet strategies.

Sex Differences
🌿

Nutraceuticals & Bioactive Compounds

We characterise dietary bioactives — myricetin, hydrogen sulphide donors, sulphur-based nutraceuticals — as therapeutic candidates for Huntington's Disease, depression, anxiety, and cognitive deficits.

Nutraceuticals
🎯

Precision Nutrition & Metabolic Therapeutics

Accounting for genetics (e.g., MTHFR polymorphisms), sex, and metabolic state, we design evidence-based precision nutrition strategies to modulate immunity in inflammatory, neurological, and metabolic disorders.

Precision Medicine

Oct 29 – Nov 1, 2025 Conference
Kumar Lab at XLIII Annual Meeting of Indian Academy of Neurosciences — IAN 2025
Ms. Arunima Das and Ms. Gauri Chaudhari attended the XLIII Annual Meeting of the Indian Academy of Neurosciences (IAN-2025) — an International Conference on Neurodevelopment and Brain Disorders: Research Trends, Technology and Translation, organised by BRIC–Rajiv Gandhi Centre for Biotechnology, Kovalam, Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala.

Both scholars had the opportunity to present their posters, receive valuable feedback from leading neuroscientists, and forge new collaborations. Gauri also participated in a hands-on Single-Cell Sequencing: Library Preparation and Data Analysis workshop, gaining practical skills in cell isolation, library preparation, data preprocessing, and single-cell clustering and visualisation using R.

A truly inspiring and skill-building experience that added meaningful value to their research journeys!

Kumar Lab at XVII European Meeting on Glial Cells in Health and Disease
Congratulations! Ms. Babita Bhatt and Ms. Chitralekha Gusain successfully presented the lab's work at the XVII European Meeting on Glial Cells in Health and Disease — Glia 2025, Marseille, France.
